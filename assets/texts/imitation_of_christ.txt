The Imitation of Christ
By Thomas à Kempis

Book One: Counsels On the Spiritual Life

Chapter 1: On Following Christ and Despising the Vanities of the World

He that followeth Me, walketh not in darkness, saith the Lord. These are the words of Christ; by which we are taught to imitate His life and manners, if we would be truly illuminated and freed from all blindness of heart.

Let therefore the life of Jesus Christ be our most fervent study. His doctrine surpasseth all the doctrines of the saints, and he that hath His spirit will find therein a hidden manna.

But many there are who hear the Gospel often and yet feel but little desire to follow it. The reason is, that they want the spirit of Jesus Christ.

Whosoever would fully and feelingly understand the words of Christ, must endeavor to conform his whole life to the life of Christ.

What will it profit thee to discourse profoundly of the Trinity, if thou lackest humility, and art thereby displeasing to the Trinity?

Verily, sublime words make not a man holy and just; but a virtuous life maketh him dear to God. I had rather feel contrition than understand its definition.

Chapter 2: On the Humble Estimation of Ourselves

All men naturally desire to know, but what good is knowledge without the fear of God?

Surely a humble husbandman that serveth God is better than a proud philosopher who, neglecting himself, occupyeth his mind in studying the course of the heavens.

He that knoweth himself well is made lowly in his own sight, and delighteth not in the praises of men.

If I understood all things in the world and had not charity, what should it profit me before God, who will judge me according to my works?

Cease from inordinate desire of knowing, for therein is found much distraction and deceit.
