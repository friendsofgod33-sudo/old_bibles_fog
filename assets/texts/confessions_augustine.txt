Confessions of St. Augustine
Book I

1. Great art Thou, O Lord, and greatly to be praised; great is Thy power, and Thy wisdom infinite. And man desires to praise Thee, for he is but a small part of Thy creation, and lo, his life is but a span, yet he bears about him his mortality, the evidence of sin, and the proof that Thou resistest the proud.

2. Yet still he desires to praise Thee, this man who is but a small portion of Thy creation. Thou hast prompted him, that he should delight to praise Thee, for Thou hast made us for Thyself, and restless is our heart until it comes to rest in Thee.

3. Grant me, O Lord, to know and understand whether first to invoke Thee or to praise Thee; and whether knowledge of Thee must precede invocation of Thee. For who can invoke Thee, not knowing Thee? For he that knoweth Thee not may invoke Thee as another than Thou art.

4. But peradventure that we may invoke Thee in order that we may come to know Thee. And how shall they call on Him in whom they have not believed? or how shall they believe on Him of whom they have not heard?

5. And they shall praise the Lord that seek Him: for as many as seek shall find Him, and finding Him shall praise Him.

Book II

1. I desire to confess before Thee, O Lord, all my wickedness, and all the mercies Thou hast shown me. Let me recall the miserable condition of my youth, when I departed far from Thee.

2. For Thou didst create me out of nothing, and didst place me in this earthly life, giving me a will to choose between good and evil. How little did I realize the terrible power of that choice!
