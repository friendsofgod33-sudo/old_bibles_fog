The Pilgrim's Progress
From This World to That Which Is to Come

Part One

As I walked through the wilderness of this world, I lighted on a certain place where was a den, and laid me down in that place to sleep: and as I slept, I dreamed a dream. I dreamed, and behold I saw a man clothed with rags, standing in a certain place, with his face from his own house, a book in his hand, and a great burden upon his back.

I looked, and saw him open the book, and read therein; and as he read, he wept, and trembled; and not being able longer to contain, he brake out with a lamentable cry, saying, "What shall I do?"

In this plight, therefore, he went home and refrained himself as long as he could, that his wife and children should not perceive his distress; but he could not be silent long, because that his trouble increased.

Wherefore at length he brake his mind to his wife, thus: "O my dear wife," said he, "I have, as thou knowest, ever since we knew each other, borne this burden of myself. But now I am convinced there is an evil coming upon us, and this burden is the token of it."

Then said his wife, "What should I do? Come, sit down, and speak to me." Then he sat down, and told her all. And she heard him out patiently.

When he had told her all, she said unto him, "Why art thou so disquieted? There must be some way of escape."

"Escape!" said he. "There is no escape but flight. But to whom shall I fly?"

Then the man said, "I know not yet; but I will seek, for I am sure deliverance is possible."

And with that he began to consider the scriptures to him, saying, "Go, and thou shalt receive instruction."

At this point the man arose up from his bed, and began to address himself to his escape.
