Practice of the Presence of God
By Brother Lawrence

The Practice of the Presence of God
Making Your Ordinary Work a Spiritual Discipline

First Conversation

The first time I saw Brother Lawrence was upon the 3rd of August, 1666. He told me his life had been a continual practice of the presence of God.

He had remarked that he had found more spiritual nourishment in common actions—in eating, drinking, and other ordinary employments—than in elaborate prayers or devotions.

"I find it is not difficult," said he, "to keep up a constant sense of the Divine presence when I attend to it, especially during the business and bustle of the kitchen, where I have many things to do at the same time. I have learned by experience that God's presence becomes most sensible to my soul when I am employed in the most troublesome and disagreeable employments."

He said that the time of business did not differ from the time of prayer; that in the noise and clatter of the kitchen, whilst several persons were calling for different things at the same time, he possessed God in as great tranquility as if he were upon his knees.

He did not ask for contemplative prayer, for the love of God, or any other gift from God. All his prayer consisted in acting everything for the love of God, and doing his ordinary business not by constraint, but with the spirit of sacrifice, and purely for the love of God.

I have sometimes found myself so caught up in that commerce and presence of God as to be unable to distinguish between work and prayer; all seemed equally holy to me when performed with love.
